# Learning Cold Dishes

## What is Cold Mixing

Cold mixing is a way to make a dish by stirring and mixing main ingredients with auxiliary ingredients.

### Forms of Cold Mixing

Cold mixing can be made into a form where main ingredients and auxiliary ingredients are mixed in space.
Cold mixing can be made into a form where main ingredients and auxiliary ingredients are separated in space, at this time auxiliary ingredients are called dipping sauce.

### Why Cold Mixing

* Some cold mixed dishes do not require heat source.
* Some cold mixing can reduce the process of washing pots (no washing or just rinse).
* Cold mixing can preserve the state of ingredients, this is especially shown in vegetables and raw meat.

### Purpose of Cold Mixing

* The purpose of cold mixing is to add flavor to tasteless or bland ingredients, such as chicken ribs.

### What Can Be Put in Cold Mixing

Including but not limited to:

* Main ingredients
* Auxiliary ingredients
* Pickling sauces
* Seasonings

### Precautions

* When cold mixing, pay attention to food safety, do not cold mix corresponding ingredients when not sure if ingredients are safe, should not cold mix corresponding ingredients when confirmed unsafe.
* Cold mixing should maximize the contact area of main ingredients, so when cold mixing, knife flowers, slicing, smashing or even crushing are recommended.
* Cold dishes put basic requirements on the stomach, please use cold mixing when confirming no diarrhea or have diarrhea time.
* When writing the file, it is under the state of COVID-19 epidemic, it is recommended to heat all ingredients in an environment above 100 degrees Celsius for more than 15 seconds for psychological comfort, if you want safety, try to avoid cold mixing.

## Utensils

Any container can be used, from porcelain jars to food-grade plastic bags.

### Precautions

* To facilitate stirring without ingredients splashing out, it is more appropriate to use a hard container with volume twice that of all ingredients.
* To ensure food safety, after pickling in plastic bags or plastic bowls, please move ingredients to porcelain or metal containers as soon as possible.
* To ensure food safety, please process raw ingredients and auxiliary ingredients on a clean cutting board.

## Process

### Sheet Vegetable Main Ingredient Processing (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

Example: Cabbage, lettuce, cabbage heart, onion, etc.

* Tear ingredients into 4cm * 4cm small pieces.
* Cut ingredients into 0.5cm long strips.
* Trim unused parts of ingredients and use whole pieces.
* Blanch processed ingredients.

#### Precautions

For some ingredients, do not peel, directly cut segments along the axis after knifing, those confident in knife skills can directly cut segments along the vertical axis.

### Block Vegetable Main Ingredient Processing (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

Example: Potatoes, water chestnuts, cucumbers, potatoes, etc.

* Cut ingredients into 0.5cm * 0.5cm cross-section strips.
* Cut ingredients into 4cm * 4cm slices less than 0.5cm thick.
* Smash or crush ingredients with knife surface (especially suitable for cucumbers).
* Use ingredients directly (especially suitable for originally small pieces).
* Blanch processed ingredients.

#### Precautions

When smashing ingredients, it may cause splashing, can wrap in food-grade plastic bag and smash.

### Block Mushroom Main Ingredient Processing (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

Example: Various mushrooms, various fungus, etc.

* Soak ingredients to soften.
* Cut ingredients into 0.5cm * 0.5cm cross-section strips.
* Cut ingredients into 4cm * 4cm slices less than 0.5cm thick.
* Use ingredients directly (especially suitable for originally small pieces).
* Blanch processed ingredients.

#### Precautions

All mushrooms need to strictly confirm safety, once on the board, everything is gone.

### Block Meat Main Ingredient Processing (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

Example: Fish meat, jellyfish head, cooked pork, cooked poultry, etc.

* Cook ingredients through steaming, boiling, roasting, frying, etc.
* Soak ingredients in cold water for a while (especially suitable for seafood).
* Tear ingredients into meat strips.
* Cut ingredients into thin slices (especially suitable for boiled pork).
* Cut ingredients into 0.8cm * 0.8cm cross-section strips.
* Directly tear or cut into large pieces by part (especially suitable for whole cooked poultry).

#### Precautions

* Pork and poultry have no exceptions, must be fully cooked, must be completely cooked, must see no blood water.
* Some beef, fish, seafood can be eaten raw after confirming safety.

### I Think This Also Counts as Ingredient Processing (This process try not to choose) (Must choose)

Example: Noodles, rice, fruits, tender leaves, etc.

* Confirm ingredient safety.
* Process ingredients into edible state.
* Process ingredients into stirrable state.

#### Precautions

* Please confirm ingredient safety, can crush a small amount of ingredients and apply on upper arm for 30 minutes to detect allergic reaction, must discard if abnormal.
* Most leaves and grasses contain ingredients that make people uncomfortable, even after cooking, please confirm safety.
* Most grains are not conducive to digestion and absorption before cooking, may have adverse effects on stomach.

### Auxiliary Ingredient Processing (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

Example: Chili peppers, garlic cloves, ginger, dried chili, etc.

* Remove stems from chili peppers, wash, cut off 0.5cm long part from stem end, then chop or cut into segments.
* Smash garlic cloves and peel, cut off 0.5cm long part from stem end, then chop or cut into segments.
* Peel ginger, then chop or shred or slice.
* Grind dried chili.
* Put prepared ingredients into small bowl or container to be used for stirring.
* Add various powders, seasonings or pickling sauces to the container, stir evenly.

#### Precautions

* The types, processing, methods of auxiliary ingredients are very broad, please do not limit your thinking, but please be careful to verify, moderate amount, remember safety.

### Mixing Ingredients (This process is optional) (Single or multiple selection)

* Squeeze out water from high water content meat ingredients and drain.
* Put high water content vegetable ingredients into container, add about 200g : 5g salt and stir, let stand 5 minutes, drain water.
* Add ingredients and auxiliary ingredients to stirring container.
* Stir with chopsticks, spoon or washed hands.
* Seal container and shake evenly.
* Tilt container to an angle where no material will spill, rotate container with axis as axis.

#### Precautions

* High water content ingredients may exude too much water after adding, diluting seasonings.
* If water is insufficient or stirring uneven during stirring, add boiled water appropriately, if unable to determine amount, 15mL each time is good.
* Some high water absorption ingredients do not recommend stirring, may cause pickled ingredients to be too salty.

### Eating (This process is required)

* Eat stirred ingredients directly.
* Dip unstirred main ingredients in dipping sauce and eat.
* Add ingredients and dipping sauce to staple food and eat.
