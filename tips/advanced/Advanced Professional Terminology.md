# Advanced Professional Terminology

Cooking is like learning programming, first you need to recognize professional terminology just like learning basic syntax, only through the combination of terminology can a dish be completed.

【Stir-fry (chǎo)】Anciently written as "煼(chǎo)", it is one of the most basic cooking methods currently; that is, cutting food into small pieces, putting them into a wok with fierce oil, quickly stirring until cooked, then adding seasonings to flavor.

【Quick-fry (qiàng)】After cutting the food, after processing with boiling water or hot oil "scald" or "soak", etc., then in the wok, explode dried chili and Sichuan pepper oil and mix well.

【Cook (chuī)】That is, using steaming, boiling, etc., to cook food. Often seen in Chaoshan cuisine.

【Boil (zhǔ)】One of the simplest cooking methods; in the wok, use appropriate amount of boiling water or soup and seasonings to cook food.

【Pan-fry (jiān)】Heat the wok, put in a little raw oil, then spread the food flat and close in the wok, use slow fire hot oil to make the surface of the food golden yellow and cooked.

【Quick-fry (bào)】Using hot wok hot oil, gather in appropriate amount of adjusted sauce or soup, make the small pieces of food in the wok quickly cooked and infused with aroma.

【Deep-fry (zhá)】Anciently written as "煠(zhá)", one of the most commonly used cooking methods; refers to putting food into a large amount of hot oil to cook until crispy.

【Boil in water (xiá)】Anciently written as "煠", using a large amount of boiling water to soften tough meat on the fire.

【Roll (gǔn)】Using the surging of a large amount of boiling water to bring out the off-flavor of food.

【Poach (cuān)】Northern cooking term, anciently "川"; almost Cantonese "渌(lù)", that is, processing food into balls or slices, cook in boiling water, then scoop into bowl, add boiling soup.

【Blanch (zhuó)】Northern written as "焯(chāo)"; refers to cutting food into thin slices, etc., using boiling water to quickly cook, then dip in sauce to eat.

【Cook in water (dá)】Put vegetables into boiling water with alum water or raw oil, cook thoroughly with slow fire, make the finished product soft and keep green.

【Hot pot (shuàn)】Northern cooking term; cut food into thin slices, put into spicy soup to cook, then dip in sauce to eat.

【Braise (kuò)】Anciently written as "爩(yù)"; refers to putting food directly into wok or clay pot, add a large amount of ginger scallion and other fragrant ingredients, cover, use a large amount of fragrant ingredients to make fragrant and reach maturity.

【Bake (jú)】Using scorching coarse salt, etc., to cook food wrapped in tin foil or parchment paper in sealed conditions.

【Stew (mèn)】Northern cooking method; refers to putting tough food into wok, add appropriate amount of soup, cover and use slow fire to soften and cook.

【Braise (wén)】Similar to northern cooking "burn", hence "southern braise northern burn"; refers to putting tough food into wok, add appropriate amount of soup, use slow fire to soften and cook.

【Stew (huì)】Use appropriate amount of soup to cook various meat and vegetables together.

【Steam (zhēng)】Use the heat of water vapor to cook food.

【Simmer (dùn)】Add water or soup to food, put into a covered container, cover, then use the heat of water vapor to cook and get soup. Northern cuisine refers to using a large amount of soup and slow fire to soften and cook food.

【Invert (kòu)】After seasoning and pre-processing food, neatly arrange into a bowl, steam over water, then invert the main ingredient into a plate and pour over the glazed sauce made from original juice.

【Braise (bāo)】Put food into a large amount of water, place on fire, slow cook and get soup.

【Simmer (áo)】Use slow fire for a long time to integrate meat flavor into soup and concentrate the soup.

【Infuse (kào)】Use strong-flavored ingredients and fresh soup, use slow fire and long time to infuse flavor into another bland main ingredient.

【Braise (wēi)】Anciently bury in charcoal ash to cook. Now refers to using ginger scallion and soup to flavor food and remove its own off-flavor. Northern cuisine also refers to putting food together with soup into a sealed clay jar, cook in slow fire.

【Smother (wù)】Replace the ancient meaning of "braise", refers to food after pickling, wrap with lotus leaf, etc., then wrap with wet mud or dough, put into charcoal fire to cook.

【Bake (hōng)】After seasoning or processing dim sum or food, put into oven to cook.

【Stir-fry (biān)】Same as煏(bì), old miswritten as "whip" or "fire convenience", similar to "熯(rǎn)", refers to putting food into hot wok, continuously stir-fry, make the moisture in food slightly dry and remove off-flavor; or through this to concentrate fresh flavor to eat.

【Velvet (liū)】Northern cooking term, similar to Cantonese "thicken", that is, using cornstarch to thicken sour-sweet sauce to make fried food tender and delicious.

【Soup (gēng)】One of the ancient cooking methods, refers to cutting food into cubes, boil with boiling soup, then add wet cornstarch, make the soup flow into paste.

【Splash (zǎn)】Once written as "溅" or "灒(zàn)", etc., divided into "splash oil" or "splash wine"; the former refers to splashing boiling hot oil on steamed food to remove fishy smell and increase smoothness; the latter refers to splashing Shaoxing wine into cooking food, make food have more "wok air".

【Scald (tàng)】Refers to using boiling water to tighten the skin of meat. Northern common refers to cooking sliced or cut ingredients in boiling soup or spicy soup.

【Roast (shāo)】Ancient "炙(zhì)", Cantonese refers to putting food on charcoal or open fire to cook. Now northern cuisine refers to using slow fire to slightly reduce juice and cook food.

【Roast (kǎo)】Northern cuisine used to replace the old meaning of "roast", hence "southern roast northern roast". Refers to putting food on open fire to cook.

【Marinate (lǔ)】Use light soy sauce and spice herbs adjusted "brine juice" to cook food or flavor it.

【Sauce (jiàng)】Use a large amount of sauce or light soy sauce to flavor or cook.

【Soak (jìn)】Use a large amount of boiling water or soup with "chrysanthemum heart" degree heat for a certain time to cook food. Similar to northern "氽(tǔn)", that is, after scalding ingredients, scoop into noodle soup to eat.

【Air-dry (fēng)】Hang pickled food in a ventilated place all year round, let it naturally dry or air-dry.

【Cure (là)】Before and after December in lunar calendar, hang pickled food in a ventilated place, let it naturally dry or air-dry.

【Smoke (yān)】Tea flavor or spice herbs ignited in sealed conditions, let food infuse with fragrant smoky flavor.

【Smoke (xūn)】Old written as "熏", divided into "dry smoke" and "wet smoke", "dry smoke" similar to "smoke"; "wet smoke" is food infused with fragrance using fresh flowers or Shaoxing wine, etc.

【Ferment (zāo)】Put food into wine lees to flavor or cook.

【Drunk (zuì)】Use a large amount of liquor to flavor or cook.

【Steam (zèng)】Ancient "steam"; cut food into pieces and season, put into clay bowl, then use strong steam to cook.

【Freeze (dòng)】Also called "crystal", refers to adding agar or pork skin to cooked food and cook into soup, then put into refrigerator to freeze and solidify to eat.

【Blanch】Put food into boiling water to half-cook and quickly scoop up, provide a good foundation for subsequent cooking.

【Ice soak】Cut food into shreds, quickly put into ice water, make food have a crisp effect. This method originates from Japan.

【Pull sugar】After food is battered and deep-fried, put into dissolved syrup and mix well, make food pull out thin threads when picked up.

【Frost】After food is deep-fried, put into dissolved syrup and mix well or directly sprinkle sugar powder.

【Salt and pepper】After food is deep-fried to cook and dry, then use pre-mixed peppercorn and salt "salt and pepper" to stir-fry and mix well.

【Oil bubble】Use a large amount of hot oil to quickly cook food.

【Pass oil】Also called "drag oil" "pass oil" "run oil"; refers to putting processed ingredients into rolling oil to quickly drag through, provide a foundation for subsequent cooking.

【Flambé】Put fresh seafood into glassware, use the heat from ignited high-proof liquor to cook.

【Zeze】Put food and ginger scallion into extremely hot clay pot, make food make "zeze" sound and emit aroma.

【Skewer】Cut meat into slices and marinate, skewer with bamboo sticks, put into hot oil to "soak" and eat. Or cut meat into slices, skewer with iron skewers, put on charcoal fire to roast, then sprinkle cumin, etc.

【Iron plate】Originally Western cooking method; that is, after "pass oil", together with onion-based fragrant ingredients and sauce, put into extremely hot iron plate to cook and make food fragrant.

【Sauna】Also called "stone cook", etc.; after pass oil, put into scorching hot stones (mostly rain flower stones), then splash adjusted sauce or soup, use steam to cook food or emit aroma.

【Pan-seal】Northern also called "pan-cook", generally suitable for fish; marinate fish with seasonings, pan-fry with hot oil slow fire, then seal with ingredients and thicken to permeate flavor.

【Nest paste】Belongs to "semi-fry" method, marinated meat coated with "nest paste" pasted on fat, use "fierce wok yin oil" to make meat crispy on one side and soft on the other.

【Nest collapse】Coat marinated food with "egg powder batter", use fry then fry method, fry food, then add seasoned fresh soup and cook through.

【Soft fry】Belongs to "semi-fry" method, mix marinated meat with "egg powder batter", use fry then fry method to cook meat, then cut and drizzle sauce.

【Egg fry】Meat first pre-cook with "blanch" or "oil bubble", then put into seasoned egg batter and mix well, then use slow fire to fry the bottom of meat egg batter to golden.

【Cutlet】Translation of English CUTLET; coat food with egg batter, stick breadcrumbs, then deep-fry in hot oil. This practice originates from Western cuisine.

【Crispy fry】Marinate food with seasonings, first coat wet batter, then pat dry cornstarch, then deep-fry in hot oil, then scoop into sauce.

【Hot pot】Also called "dip pot", Guangdong called "side stove", fresh meat cut into thin slices, or meat made into balls, dumplings, etc., together with vegetables, etc., sent to guests, let guests put into boiling water or soup to cook themselves.

【Steam pot】Marinate meat, together with herbs, put into a special "steam pot" with boiling seasoned soup, simmer finely to eat.

【Cold mix】Cut cooked food or fruits and vegetables, add seasonings and mix well.

【Fish sashimi】Remove blood from fresh seafood, cut into thin slices, mix with ginger shreds, scallion shreds, thin crisps, lemon shreds, etc., then dip light soy sauce to eat.

【Sashimi】Originally Japanese cuisine, originally refers to eating raw meat slices, after Chinese cuisine adoption refers to fresh seafood or seafood descaled and blooded, thinly sliced, drizzled with lemon juice, dipped with Japanese mustard to eat.

【Bamboo tube】Anciently called "熷(zēng)", refers to using bamboo tube as utensil, then through "roast" "burn" "steam" "stew" etc. to cook food.

【Honey sauce】Refers to melting white sugar, honey, maltose, etc. into thick juice, put into processed ingredients, through "simmer" "steam" etc. to make texture soft and glutinous, sweet permeate, moisten through sugar juice.

【Blanch】Also called "out water", is a method of preliminary cooking by putting ingredients in boiling water or cold water pot.

【Pass oil】Use oil as heat transfer medium to preliminary cook ingredients. Small ingredients pass through warm oil called "slide oil"; large ingredients pass through fierce oil called "pass oil".

【Batter】Before cooking, evenly coat ingredients with a layer of batter.

【Coat】Mix ingredients with cornstarch, eggs, salt, etc., coat the outer layer with a thin layer of batter.

【Toughen】Add salt, water, cornstarch and other auxiliary ingredients to processed animal ingredients ground into paste, repeatedly stir to reach bright color, fine texture, sink in water, not disperse state.

【Thicken】In the cooking process, add cornstarch solution to the pot, make the dish soup have a certain thickness. Also called "stick greasy", "stick thicken", "gather thicken".

【Warm oil】Commonly called three to four percent, temperature generally 70℃~100℃.

【Hot oil】Commonly called five to six percent, temperature generally 110℃~170℃.

【Fierce oil】Commonly called seven to eight percent, temperature generally 180℃~220℃.

【Slide wok】Heat the wok, drizzle a little oil to slide the wok, then pour out the oil.

【Sizzle wok】Also called "fry wok", refers to putting ginger, scallion, chili powder or other fragrant seasonings into heated bottom oil, wok, stir-fry to fragrant, then timely add ingredients.

【Superior broth】Also called "clear soup", "superior soup", "top soup", refers to broth made from pig bones, chicken bones, duck frames, minced meat heads, etc., add chicken paste or meat paste adjusted to thin paste without salt, make it clear as water, rich and fresh.

【Cream soup】Also called "white soup", ingredients added with water to braise into milky white soup.
