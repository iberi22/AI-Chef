# Unveiling the Wisdom of Food Pairings: These Foods Should Not Be Eaten Together

In daily cooking, we all hope to make delicious and healthy home-cooked dishes. However, some seemingly ordinary ingredients may hide "mysteries" when paired together, not only affecting the color, aroma, and taste of the food, but also possibly hindering nutrient absorption, or even subtly affecting physical health. Understanding these "food incompatibilities" and "eating taboos" is an important step in improving dietary wisdom and protecting family health.

## Common Food Pairing Misconceptions and Scientific Interpretations

Here are some food combinations that need special attention on our dining table:

1. **Spinach + Tofu: The "Clash" of Oxalic Acid and Calcium**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Spinach is rich in oxalic acid, while tofu is a good source of calcium. When eaten together, oxalic acid combines with calcium ions to form insoluble calcium oxalate.
    * **Possible Impact**: Calcium oxalate is not only difficult for the body to absorb and utilize, but long-term large intake may also increase the risk of stones.
    * **Health Advice**: Before cooking spinach, it is recommended to blanch it in boiling water first, which can effectively remove most of the oxalic acid, thereby reducing its combination with calcium.

2. **Carrots + Radishes: The "Depleter" of Vitamin C**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Carrots contain a special "ascorbic acid oxidase" (i.e., vitamin C decomposing enzyme), which destroys vitamin C in other foods.
    * **Possible Impact**: Causes a large loss of vitamin C in radishes (and other vitamin C-rich foods, such as citrus fruits), reducing their nutritional value.
    * **Health Advice**: It is best to eat them separately, or cook the carrots before eating them with vitamin C-rich foods, because high temperatures will inactivate the enzyme.

3. **Shrimp + Large Amounts of Vitamin C: Potential Risk, But No Need to Panic Excessively**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Shrimp and other shellfish contain a "pentavalent arsenic" compound. Under the reducing action of extremely high doses of vitamin C, pentavalent arsenic may theoretically be reduced to highly toxic "trivalent arsenic" (commonly known as arsenic).
    * **Possible Impact**: Theoretically poisoning, but **please note**: The daily intake of shrimp and vitamin C in normal diet is far from enough to cause poisoning. This is an exaggerated "incompatibility", no need to panic excessively.
    * **Health Advice**: Normal diet is fine, no need to deliberately avoid. Avoid ingesting large amounts at once.

4. **Persimmons + Crabs: The "Double Test" of the Digestive Tract**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Persimmons are rich in tannic acid (also known as tannin), while crabs are high in protein. Tannic acid easily coagulates with protein into indigestible lumpsâ€”tannin protein.
    * **Possible Impact**: May cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as bloating, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting, and even aggravate constipation.
    * **Health Advice**: Try to avoid eating together, or at least separate by several hours. Those with spleen and stomach deficiency should pay special attention.

5. **Milk + Chocolate: The "Invisible Obstacle" to Calcium Absorption**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Chocolate contains oxalic acid, which combines with calcium in milk to form calcium oxalate.
    * **Possible Impact**: Affects calcium absorption, reducing the calcium supplementation effect of milk.
    * **Health Advice**: It is recommended to eat them separately, or after a period of time.

6. **Soy Milk + Eggs: The "Digestive Challenge" of Protein**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Uncooked soy milk contains a trypsin inhibitor, which affects the body's digestion and absorption of protein.
    * **Possible Impact**: Reduces the utilization rate of egg protein, may cause indigestion.
    * **Health Advice**: Ensure that the soy milk is thoroughly boiled and cooked (false boiling does not count), then pair it with eggs, so that the trypsin inhibitor is destroyed and will not have adverse effects.

7. **Cucumbers + Tomatoes: The "Silent Loss" of Vitamin C**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Similar to carrots, cucumbers also contain a vitamin C decomposing enzyme.
    * **Possible Impact**: Destroys vitamin C in foods like tomatoes, reducing their antioxidant and immune-enhancing effects.
    * **Health Advice**: It is best to eat them separately. If making a salad, consider eating tomatoes first, then cucumbers, or process them separately.

8. **Lamb + Watermelon: The "Collision" of Cold and Heat**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Lamb is warm in nature, with the effect of replenishing deficiency and dispelling cold; watermelon is cold and cool, with the effect of clearing heat and relieving summer heat.
    * **Possible Impact**: Eating both together, the cold and hot properties conflict, may cause spleen and stomach discomfort, causing diarrhea, bloating and other digestive problems, especially for those with weak spleen and stomach.
    * **Health Advice**: Avoid eating large amounts in the same meal.

9. **Pork + Tea: The "Obstacle" to Protein Absorption**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: Tea contains tannic acid, which combines with protein in pork to form indigestible precipitates.
    * **Possible Impact**: Affects the digestion and absorption of protein, may cause constipation or indigestion.
    * **Health Advice**: Drink tea one hour after meals, or avoid drinking large amounts of strong tea when eating meat.

10. **Honey + Tofu: Digestive "Incoordination"**
    * **Incompatibility Principle**: The organic acids in honey combine with the protein in tofu, possibly forming indigestible substances.
    * **Possible Impact**: May cause gastrointestinal discomfort, such as diarrhea.
    * **Health Advice**: Try to avoid eating together.

## Scientifically View "Incompatibilities", Wisely Pair Daily Diet

* **"Incompatibilities" Are Not Absolute Taboos**: Most so-called "food incompatibilities" have not been found in scientific research to cause serious poisoning or fatal consequences. Many are based on traditional experience, minority cases, or in vitro experiment speculations. Eating small amounts or occasional pairings in daily life usually do not have obvious effects on health.
* **Focus on Balanced Diversity**: The principle of healthy diet is balance and diversification. Rather than worrying excessively about "incompatibilities", it is better to pay attention to the rationality of the overall diet structure, avoid partial eating and picky eating.
* **Cooking Methods Matter**: Some "incompatibility" problems can be avoided or alleviated through appropriate cooking methods (such as blanching, high-temperature heating).
* **Individual Differences Are Large**: Each person's constitution, digestive ability, and sensitivity to food are different. Combinations that may cause discomfort for some people may have no effect on others.
* **Pay Attention to Your Own Feelings**: If you feel uncomfortable after eating a certain combination, pay attention and avoid it next time.
* **Special Groups Please Consult Professionals**: If you have special health conditions, chronic diseases (such as diabetes, kidney disease, etc.), or a history of allergies to certain foods, be sure to consult a doctor or registered dietitian for more targeted and personalized dietary advice.
